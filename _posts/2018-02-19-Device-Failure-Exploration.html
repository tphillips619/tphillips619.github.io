Business Understanding¶

Building a model to predict the probability of device failures in a fleet of vehicles.

A company has a fleet of devices transmitting daily aggregated telemetry attributes. The current technique of scheduling maintenance at a predetermined interval of time can keep working devices out of service during scheduled maintenance time and miss other devices that fail while in service. The goal is to use the attributes of this dataset to build a binary classifier that can predict the occurence of device failures. This can mitigate revenue lost by keeping working devices off the road during scheduled maintenance and improve the likelihood of catching vehicles from being on the road when they fail.

The values of attribute6 range from 8 to 689,161. 'attribute6' values are higher than what you would expect to see as an odometer reading for a passenger vehicle. A friend and truck driver reported to me that repairs and maintenance that would be uneconomical to perform on a passenger vehicle (e.g. replacing the engine) may be economical for a truck. A truck could potentially be running with all original parts repaired or replaced, allowing a truck to have a higher than expected odometer reading. This makes it feasible for attribute6 to represent mileage despite the higher than expected odometer readings.

If the attribute is mileage, it should be monotonically increasing. If it is not, it may not be mileage, or there could be errors in the dataset

Part of the challenge of using this dataset is the need to make educated guesses about what each feature represents.

  • Attribute1 -> Consider removing, it has a uniform distribution and is unique for almost every observation
  • Attribute2 -> 558 unique values, Numerical
  • Attribute3 -> 47 unique values, could be Categorical
  • Attribute4 -> 115 unique values, Numerical
  • Attribute5 -> Numerical, Approximately normal with positive Skew, does not have mode of '0'
  • Attribute6 -> Numerical, Approximately normal, Label as 'mileage'
  • Attribute7 -> 28 unique values, Categorical
  • Attribute8 -> Remove (redundant attribute)
  • Attribute9 -> Numerical

More information about the provenance of these features would help make better inferences. I'll make an educated guess that non-zero attributes will be important in building a predictive model. Creating a feature that separates zero values from non-zero values would be a great place to start.

Given how rare a failure is in this dataset, I'll have to focus on feature engineering tasks and models that can handle predicting rare events. Decision tree based algorithms are capable of finding combinations of features, are robust to class imbalances, and are the type of models that I'll use to produce a classifier capable of predicting that not all devices will remain working.
